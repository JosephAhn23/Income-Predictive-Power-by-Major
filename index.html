Median Starting Salaries (Computer Science)
Canadian Citizen Outcomes — 2025 USD

Source: College Transitions Dataverse

Core Formula (Canadian Citizen Median, 2025 USD)

Canadian Median = C + 0.5 × p × (U − C)

Where:

C = 75,000 (Canada CS market median, 2025 USD)

p = 0.30 (share working in the U.S. by year ~2)

U = USDOE_2023 × 1.061

Combined Single-Line Formula:
Canadian Median = 75,000 + 0.5 × 0.30 × ((USDOE_2023 × 1.061) − 75,000)
Google Sheets / Excel Formula:
=75000 + 0.5*0.30*((C2*1.061)-75000)
With Optional Rounding:
=ROUND(75000 + 0.5*0.30*((C2*1.061)-75000), -3)

General Engineering — Model Assumptions (Canadian Citizen)

Engineering Median = Cₑ + 0.5 × pₑ × (Uₑ − Cₑ)

Where:

Cₑ = 70,000 (Canada general engineering median, 2025 USD)

pₑ = 0.25 (share working in the U.S. by year ~2)

Uₑ = USDOE_2023 Engineering × 1.061

Combined Single-Line Formula:
Engineering Median = 70,000 + 0.5 × 0.25 × ((USDOE_2023 Engineering × 1.061) − 70,000)
Google Sheets / Excel Formula:
=70000 + 0.5*0.25*((D2*1.061)-70000)
(where D2 is the USDOE Engineering 2023 median)
With Optional Rounding:
=ROUND(70000 + 0.5*0.25*((D2*1.061)-70000), -3)

⚠️ Missing Input Data: To calculate Engineering medians, we need the USDOE_2023 Engineering median for each institution. Currently, we only have Computer Science data. Once you have the Engineering salary data from College Transitions or USDOE, the formula above can calculate the Canadian Citizen medians.

Note: Engineering has a lower Canadian anchor (70k vs 75k for CS) and lower U.S. pull (0.25 vs 0.30 for CS) due to fewer TN-eligible roles, more licensing requirements, and less U.S. startup/Big Tech pull early in career.
